Ellora Caves

The Ellora Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest rock-cut cave complexes in the world. Built between 600–1000 AD, the caves represent Hindu, Buddhist and Jain traditions.

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • 34 rock-cut caves
  • Built between 600–1000 AD
  • Hindu, Buddhist & Jain monuments
  • Kailasa Temple is most famous
  • Carved from single rock mountain

A masterpiece of ancient Indian rock-cut architecture.

Kailasa Temple – Cave 16

The Kailasa Temple is the most remarkable structure at Ellora. It is the world’s largest monolithic rock excavation, carved entirely from a single stone. It was built by Rashtrakuta king Krishna I.

Religious Harmony

Ellora contains 17 Hindu caves, 12 Buddhist caves, and 5 Jain caves built close together. This shows the religious harmony that existed in ancient India.

Location

Ellora Caves are located 29 km from Sambhajinagar and are protected by the Archaeological Survey of India. Today, it is one of Maharashtra’s biggest tourist attractions.
