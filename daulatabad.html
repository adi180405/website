Daulatabad Fort

Originally known as Devagiri, this powerful hill fort was built in the 12th century by the Yadava dynasty. It later became the capital of the Delhi Sultanate under Muhammad bin Tughluq.

  • Originally called Devagiri
  • Built by Yadava ruler Bhillama V
  • Capital shifted here in 1327
  • Strong defense with secret tunnels
  • Famous Chand Minar inside the fort
  • Located 15 km from Sambhajinagar

Architecture & History

The fort stands on a 200-meter-high conical hill with vertical carved rock walls to prevent climbing. It has only one entrance, curved pathways, false doors, and spike-studded gates designed to confuse enemies.
